Ancient Celtic place names in Cantabria
    ​

    Cantabria *kanto- "stone" or *canto- “rim, border”, briga “hill-fort”

    ​

    Deva flumen *deiwo "divine" or "goddess"

    ​

    Magrada flumen *mag- “field” and *ratis “earthen wall, fortified settlement”. Very similar is the Old Irish toponym Mag Ratha (“field of the fort”)

    ​

    Saunium flumen Cornish sawn “cleft, gully”

    ​

    Vindius mons *windo- “white”

    ​

    Aracelium *ar(a)- “raised”, *celo- “hill”

    ​

    Attica Unknown, cognate to Celtiberian Attacum and Gaulish Atica

    ​

    Cambarica *cambo- “crooked” (Old Irish camb)

    ​

    Dracina Gaulish drageno- “thorn” (Old Irish draigen, Cornish and Middle Welsh draen)

    ​

    Moroeca/Morogi *mor(i)- “sea” (Gaulish mori-, Welsh and Cornish mor)

    ​

    Ocella *ocelo- “promontory”

    ​

    Vellica *wel- “to choose, wish”, *wello- “better”, with Celtic adjectival suffix -ico-. (chosen place)

    ​

    Veseiasueca Celtic *weis- “to soak, flow”

    Gabalaeca Celtic *gabalo- “fork” (Gallo-Roman gabalus “javelin, gallows”, Old Irish gabul “forked branch, bifurcation”, from IE *ghabolo- “fork”) plus a suffix -aika

    ​

    Segontia Paramica *sego- “victory” with IE toponymic suffix -ntia

    ​

    Tenobrica *teno- “to stretch, extend”, cf. Cornish ten “stretched”, or from Celtic *tep-no- “hot”. briga “hill-fort”

    ​

    Tulonium Welsh twlch “hillock”
